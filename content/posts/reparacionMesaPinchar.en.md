I have always loved mixing music, and one of my dreams is to become a **DJ**. The problem is that good mixers are expensive.

My first mixer was a **Traktor**. At one party someone spilled a drink on it, and the next day it was basically dead. I disassembled it and kept it for spare parts.

---

Years later, a friend gave me a damaged **Pioneer** with several faulty buttons. I tried to repair it, but my desoldering skills were limited, and I damaged several solder pads.

I had to scrape copper traces with a needle and solder replacement parts from the old Traktor. It was messy, but it works.

Main lesson: **soldering temperature matters** if you do not want to destroy your PCB. I definitely need a better soldering station.
