A colleague of mine is launching a **car rental company**, and I proposed making **custom keychains** as a marketing asset. I had already helped with his logo, brand registration, photos, and custom plate frames. Then we came up with this keychain idea.

---

## Design ideas

We settled on two keychain concepts:

1. A **round keychain** with the brand initials, **LR**.
2. A **license-plate keychain** to identify each car.

Since the logo has two colors and my printer is single-color, I designed the keychain in separate parts and glued them after printing.

{{< figure src="images/lr2.jpg" alt="Keychains" caption="Black and White Keychains" class="webp" loading="lazy" width="600" >}}

---

### Printing process

- **Logo keychain**:
  I printed a black circular base with grooves for white letters. Due to 3D printing tolerances, I had to run several iterations to get a proper fit.

- **License-plate keychain**:
  This one was trickier because I wanted the same typography as real Spanish plates. I designed the text in Photoshop, converted it to STL with [Image to STL](https://imagetostl.com/convert/file/png/to/stl), then extruded and adjusted it in OnShape.

{{< figure src="images/lr1.jpg" alt="Keychains" caption="Keychain and Plate" class="webp" loading="lazy" width="600">}}

---

### Final details

We bought key rings from AliExpress because the cost was much lower and quality was equivalent to local hardware stores.

---

### Next steps

The keychains turned out great. My next idea is to add an **NFC sticker** to the logo keychain, so customers can tap with their phone and open a **landing page** with links to the rental site and social media.
